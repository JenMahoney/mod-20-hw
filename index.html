Mobile App Case Study: Wheel Wise

The goal of this project was to research, prototype, test, and validate an idea for a new mobile application.

Project Timeline

3 Weeks

My Role

UX Researcher & UI Designer (Group Project)

Team Members

Andrew Kellogg, Nick Sucik, Pangkou Vang, Allen Xiong

Tools Used

Figma, FigJam, Photoshop, Google Drive/Sheets/Slides

Project Overview

The problem

Many car owners neglect their automobiles care because of the headache that comes with overwhelming amounts of resources without much community knowledge.

Initial solution

Create an app for car enthusiasts to connect with others who share their interests and share their knowledge.

(After a lot of team debate, reviewing data from our initial interviews, and eliminating scope creep, we turned away from the car enthusiast community and began exploring what an app for the average car user might look like.)

Revised solution

Create an app for the average car user that helps them track maintenance and find good deals on service in their area. This could be useful to an car owner whether they do their own maintenance or take it to a mechanic.

WheelWise is your trusted companion to stay ahead of car maintenance tasks without the stress and uncertainty of not knowing what you don't know. Car maintenance is a complex world where you often have to take the experts' word, and trust they have your best interest in mind. Our app aims to empower the average car owner with the knowledge to stay on top of their maintenance and aims to keep their vehicle running at peak performance for years to come.

Research Phase

Research Plan

Research goal

As user researchers, we want to understand the needs, preferences, and pain points of the new and existing car owners to inform the development of the WheelWise app.

Objective 1

To investigate whether individuals associate their type of car with their personal identity or “brand” and the extent to which this association influences their self-perception and social image.

Objective 2

To investigate the factors that influence individuals' maintenance behavior, including personal preferences, perceived importance of car care, and the perceived reliability or performance of their specific car model.

Objective 3

To explore the unique relationship people have with their cars, understand the role cars play in their lives and explore how we can enhance that through building new shared relationships through the app.

Methodology

(Allen had the initial idea for our app, so he conducted the majority of our interviews. He looked for a mix of car enthusiasts and more “regular” car owners amongst his friends.” Andrew also conducted one interview.)

Key Takeaways
  • A lot of people use their cars everyday for simple tasks (work, errands, etc.)
  • These car owners prefer to do maintenance themselves, if it is feasible for them to do so, in order to save money.
  • We found that many car owners feel a sense of camaraderie with others based on their general shared interest in cars vs the assumption that they would feel strongly towards those with the same car as them.
  • We found a mix of those who feel their car is an extension of their identity vs their car being a tool to get around.
  • Many people have reservations about trusting information they find along and look for a variety of sources for car info.

Definition and Ideation Phase

User Insight

Car owners need a community of other car owners to feel more equipped to take care of their cars, because of the overload of multiple sources and auto shops that can be found online can be unreliable and confusing.

Affinity Diagram

I took the interview transcripts and created the sticky notes for the affinity diagram. Our group then came together to review and revise the categories.

Here we learned about some of the users' needs, including cost-efficient maintenance and basic habits of the car owners. We learned some of the places they go for info and how they go about maintaining their vehicle.

Key takeaways from the affinity diagram are below.

Feelings Towards Maintenance
  • Feels routine maintenance is important to increase longevity of car
  • Says an app would help them stay on top of maintenance needs
Approach to Maintenance
  • Prefers to do basic maintenance themself to save money
  • Willing to pay if too complex or time consuming
  • Gets help from family if available
Information Sources
  • Prefers to get info from knowledgable friends/family first
  • Will look online for info if needed
  • Thinks it is important to look at multiple online sources
  • Will go to mechanic if needed
Maintenance Habits
  • Keeps track of routine services
  • Keeps to recommended schedule
  • Will put off some things a short time if money is tight

Revised User Insight

Car owners feel strongly that routine maintenance is key to the long term health of their vehicle. The average car user wants assistance to track upcoming maintenance tasks and save money on repairs to optimize the performance and longevity of their vehicle.

Problem Statement

We are designing WheelWise to help everyday car owners to have access to car knowledge, so they can either take care of maintenance themselves or know how much a good price would be. We have observed that many car owners don’t have proper community or guidance with auto maintenance, causing them to neglect taking care of their cars. This could be both hazardous and expensive to the car owner, and could happen anytime on the road. How might we build an app so that car owners are proactive with their auto maintenance?

I like, I wish, what if...

We used insights from user interviews and the affinity diagram to brainstorm potential features through an "I like, I wish, What if" exercise. After dot voting on our favorites, I grouped them into four categories and we conducted another round of dot voting to identify our top three priorities.

This last round of dot voting was the final decision to move away from the forum and community aspect and focus on the maintenance and cost savings potential in our app.

Identity/Community
  • I like sharing photos of my car and seeing what others have done to theirs
  • What if the app alerted you when you were near other users with shared interests?
Maintenance
  • I like knowing my car is up to date on maintenance
  • I like when my car runs smoothly with no problems
Money
  • I like saving money on auto care and maintenance
Knowledge
  • I wish there was a centralized platform for car knowledge
  • What if the app was integrated with the tech in my car?

Value Proposition

WheelWise is an app to help average car owners stay on top of routine maintenance. It's better because it will provide peace of mind by notifying users when upcoming maintenance tasks are due. It's believable because our automatic alerts ensure you never forget another routine service, ultimately saving you money by keeping your car running smoothly.

Prototyping and Testing Phase

User Flow

Our initial user flow began with options for users to sign up or log in to the app. Next, users were prompted to enable notifications and location sharing. After this, they were directed to the home screen, where they could add their car details.

We used this flow to create sketch wireframes. Each team member developed and tested their own paper prototypes. I combined the most positively received features into our revised user flow and mid-fi wireframes.

Features added to the home screen include local discount alerts, a mileage tracker, and a maintenance detail snapshot. We also created a separate details page.

User Flow Steps

  • Choice to log in or sign up
    • If new account - asked for GPS permissions
    • If new account - asked to allow notifications
  • Home screen
  • Add vehicle info
  • Add maintenance info
  • Home screen (now with description of car)

Sketch Wireframes

Another teammate and I combined features from the paper prototypes and created the mid-fi wireframes. We then ran these by the group for feedback and moved onto the user testing plan.

Revised User Flow

We revised our user flow based on feedback from our paper prototype tests.

Changes to User Flow Steps
  • Focused on new account path
    • This allowed us to highlight more of our functions vs an existing account where the user has already accepted all the permissions
    • If new account - asked to allow notifications
  • Added a clear reason for needing location permission
    • Based on the feature prioritization matrix and what we learned in the interviews we decided to build out the local deals feature
  • Moved permissions requests back further in the process
    • This gives users context for why they are being asked to share their info
    • Notification permission screen now appears after adding first car
    • Location sharing now appears when clicking find local deals
  • Add maintenance info
  • Home screen (now with description of car)

    • Mid-fi Wireframes

    Our wireframes show our user starting with creating an account with an email. Which takes our user to the home screen after creating an account. From the home screen the user gets to choose if the user wants to make a decision in finding local discount or adding a vehicle to the app. If the user decides to find local discounts, it will then ask a notification to allow GPS to find nearby shops. Either answer chosen (YES or No) takes the user back to the home page. From the home page, the user will now add a vehicle, the user will input car info (car name, model, make, etc). App will ask the user to make sure all info is correct before proceeding forward. Once info is confirmed, the user will now input car maintenance info (mileage, oil change, spark plug, etc.) now the user can hit add and allow for notifications if wanted to know maintenance done for the vehicle.

    Final Thoughts and Next Steps

    With this project we were developed a streamlined approach for every day car users to stay on top of their auto work and maintenance. We developed an easy notification-based process that has a simple and easy to understand interface.

    In the future we would like to implement more of a community aspect within the application to connect car owners with one another for topics like DIY maintenance and a hub for camaraderie and knowledge sharing. We would also like to finish implementing the miles tracker and have a rating system for local shops based on certain criteria in the area.

    (If I were to continue this project I would also take into account the feedback we got from our presentation. In initial group discussions we brought up the possibility of connecting the app to a diagnostic tool as well as CarFax which would both be great additions to the functionality of the app, and were both brought up by classmates. I thought the comment about the sports car image on the prototype being off putting to those who own older cars to be a really good point. I would remove that image and replace it with more of an abstract car image.)
